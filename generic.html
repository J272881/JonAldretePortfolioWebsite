Customer Purchase Prediction

In this assignment, we used a data set of information about 45,211 customers, including demographic information and data related to their experience with the bank and previous marketing campaigns. Additionally, we have a class variable "y" that indicates whether this customer purchased a term product in the most recent marketing campaign. We aim to predict which customers will buy a term product if we spend the money to advertise to them. We want to develop a model that will maximize the returns based on marketing costs and the benefits of customer purchases.

To do this, we used two classification models, the decision tree Model and the Naive Bayes model, then evaluated their results using the confusion matrix. We used a cost-benefit analysis to calculate the net profit of each model. After comparing the net profit, we concluded that using the decision tree model was the best method for this situation.
